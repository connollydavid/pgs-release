# Marcus: the Media-Library Archivist

*The one-command converter.*

**Modality: embodied and goal-driven.** Maintains a family media server in the
evenings; comfortable in a terminal but not a compiler; finds tools through
search and keeps the first one that works. Judges software in the first five
minutes.

- **Goals:** turn PGS and VobSub tracks into SRT his media server can search
  and restyle; convert in his own language and his parents' language, not only
  English; download a binary he can trust and run it the same way on his NAS
  and his laptop.
- **Frustrations:** builds that need a dependency chain before the first
  conversion; OCR that silently mangles non-Latin scripts; forced-subtitle
  flags lost in conversion so foreign dialogue stops appearing.
- **Works by:** downloading from the website, pasting one command from the
  docs, spot-checking three subtitles in the middle of the film, then batch
  converting the library.
- **Scenario:** Marcus rips a boxed set, runs the documented ffmpeg one-liner
  over every .sup file, and gets timed SRT out. The two discs with Japanese
  tracks come out readable, and the forced track stays forced in the output.
